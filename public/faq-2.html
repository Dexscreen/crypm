Cryptocurrency mining electricity consumption varies significantly by hardware type:

  • ASIC Bitcoin Miners: 3,000-3,500 watts (72-84 kWh daily)
  • GPU Mining Rig (6 cards): 1,200-1,800 watts (29-43 kWh daily)
  • Single GPU: 200-350 watts (5-8 kWh daily)
  • CPU Mining: 65-150 watts (1.5-3.6 kWh daily)

For comparison, an average US household uses about 30 kWh daily. A single ASIC miner uses 2-3 times more electricity than an entire home. This is why electricity cost is the primary factor determining mining profitability.

Many profitable mining operations are located in regions with cheap renewable energy, such as hydroelectric power in Washington State or geothermal energy in Iceland.
